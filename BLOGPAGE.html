ART

What comes to your mind when you hear the word Art ?

a painting ?

a sketch ?

a wall mural maybe ?


But that's not what art is all about.

In my opinion, ART is a generalized term for anything that you love doing.

something that makes you feel good on your bad days,
something that you're not forced to do by someone,
something that you discover yourself while growing up,
something that keeps calling you when you're bored,
something that you call your form of therapy,
something that comes from within.

THAT'S ART.

For me that something is sketching and painting.

HAVING A HOBBY - AN ART WITHIN

Everyone has some form of ART present within them, maybe they develop it over the years, or it's just there.

someone may love penning down their thoughts and turn it into poetry,
someone may love to hold a brush and turn the canvas into a beautiful landscape,
someone may love to dance like no one's watching,
someone may love to fix the broken pieces together- I'm talking about jigsaw puzzles xD
someone may love to capture every little detail around them,
someone may love quizzing, singing, cooking, and whatnot.

These are the forms of art that we know, the list never ends though.
What's important is, having a hobby.
Having something 

 for yourself
that no one can ever take away from you.

ART v/s SKILL

Any artist needs to have a throbbing heart, an opinion, courage to express it and imagination to choose his/her own form of expression. With all these whatever he/she creates becomes art, not otherwise. His/her way of expression becomes style. Some revelations lead to revolutions, either in the form of expression or in real life. Some revelations lead to realization.

Art takes much more than just skill.
Patience and enjoyment are the two key factors that can do wonders, even if you don't have the required skill.

ART AS THERAPY

Art therapy is a therapeutic approach that promotes healing by tapping into and revealing an individual's deepest thoughts and feelings through art.
Often, we feel therapy is given to someone having any disorder or undergoing depression.
But, the underlying truth is that we all need therapy and the best part is we can choose our form of therapy.

Art should be treated as therapy because it helps us
to give time to ourselves,
to calm down our racing thoughts,
to express our feelings and emotions,
to discover ourselves, and
to feel better.

"Art washes from the soul the dust of everyday life." ~ Pablo Picasso

Art enables us to lose ourselves
and find ourselves
at the same time.
